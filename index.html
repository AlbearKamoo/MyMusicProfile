Welcome to my Music Profile!


Music is a big part of who I am.

It is my greatest form of creative expression, and always provides me with exciting and novel experiences.

I got really into music when I became the vocalist for a pre-teen rock band when I was 10 years old. Since then I have learned a ton of music theory and how to play two instruments! Check out the details of my musical identity below :)


What I Play

Epiphone

An Epiphone Les Paul Custom electric guitar with a silverburst finish. My pride and joy.

Fender

A Fender steel-string acoustic guitar. My first big purchase upon going to college and forever my pick up and play partner.

Guitar

I started playing guitar when I was 12 years old, jamming on my dad's old acoustic. The first songs I learned how to play were "Paranoid" by Black Sabbath and "Smoke on Water" by Deep Purple (both songs that I did vocals for in my band). Shortly after, my dad bought me my first electric guitar and I began taking lessons. It was rough at first, but I emerged with an extensive knowledge of both techincal skills and theory.

Today guitar is my go-to instrument for whenever I need some emotional exhaust, or just need to ponder something. Also, whenever I hear a new song I enjoy I usually tend to pick up my acoustic and attempt to decipher the riffs and chord progressions by ear.

Keyboard

Piano/Keyboard used to be something that I would always sheepishly appreciate from afar. I loved the instrument's unique sound and aesthetics, but aside from some simple tunes I knew nothing about how to play it.

Fast Forward to 2012. My dad decided to take keyboard lessons, and promply bought a nice keyboard for the household. I was excited, but also intimidated by the prospect of having to learn how to play a whole other instrument. It was very slow going since I had no idea how to go about it. It took me about a year and a half to get to the point where I could successfully play "To Zanarkand" from Final Fantasy X, one of my favorite piano pieces.

That was a huge breakthrough point for me; after that I started playing regurlarly and gradually improved. Today I have a keyboard at my own place, and I am just starting to tackle some more advanced techiniques.

My very own Yamaha P-115!

Orchestral

Notice I didn't write 'Classical'. Although I do appreciate some Chopin and Vivaldi here and there, the 'Orchestral' genre I refer to encompasses musical scores written for an orchestral ensemble of classic instruments, but that aren't of the classical style itself. Examples of this are movie soundtracks and modern scores by artists such as Two Steps From Hell.
